Optimizing GPU Clusters for AI Training Workloads

2025-03-01 8 min read

As AI models grow in complexity, the infrastructure supporting them must evolve. Training a modern LLM is no longer just about having 'enough' GPUs; it's about how those GPUs communicate.

The Bottleneck: Interconnectivity

In large-scale AI training, the biggest bottleneck isn't the compute power of a single card, but the latency between them. Technologies like NVIDIA's NVLink and high-bandwidth networking (InfiniBand or RoCE) are essential.

Storage Throughput

Feeding a GPU cluster requires a storage layer that can keep up. Standard cloud storage often fails here. We recommend parallel file systems like Lustre or specialized high-performance NVMe solutions.

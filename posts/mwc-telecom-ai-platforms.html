MWC 2026: telecoms want to stop being simple networks and become platforms for AI

2026-03-07 4 min read

Beyond the specific announcements, MWC Barcelona 2026 left a structural reading about the future of the telecom sector. Operators and major technology providers are trying to leave behind the role of simple data carriers to become platforms that enable artificial intelligence, programmable services, edge computing and new digital models.

During the closing of the congress it was highlighted that the industry is entering a new phase in which telecommunications integrate with cloud, data and AI ecosystems. This means that value is no longer just in offering connectivity, but in participating more actively in the services and automation layer.

An economic necessity

This evolution also responds to an economic necessity. If networks will support ever more intelligent traffic, more distributed inference and more critical applications, telecoms need more open, programmable and profitable infrastructures. Hence why the MWC 2026 discourse focused so much on architecture, efficiency and orchestration.

The network as a distributed computing platform

For businesses, this could translate into networks more adaptable to specific needs, greater integration between connectivity and operations, and new services with embedded intelligence. For the sector, the conclusion is even more profound: the network is no longer a passive channel and begins to behave as a distributed computing platform.

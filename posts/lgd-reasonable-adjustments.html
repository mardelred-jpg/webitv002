Reasonable adjustments and adaptations: an obligation that many companies overlook

2025-03-03 4 min read

Hiring is only one part of compliance. Once a person with a disability enters a selection process or a job, the legal framework also requires adaptations and reasonable adjustments when needed.

What are reasonable adjustments?

Reasonable adjustments are practical changes that allow a person with a disability to participate on equal terms. In public employment, the rules expressly include adaptations of time and means in selection processes, support measures and workplace adaptations once the person joins the role.

Why this matters in private environments too

Although the attached rules on access exams focus on public employment, they reflect a broader legal principle already present in disability law: equality is not real if the process is formally open but materially inaccessible. For businesses, this is especially relevant in interviews, tests, onboarding and digital tools.

A practical compliance checkpoint

Companies should review whether their hiring channels, forms, technical tests, internal communications and job environments create avoidable barriers. Very often, the legal issue is not refusal, but failure to adapt.

The Startup Guide to LGD Compliance in Spain

2025-03-05 5 min read

Reaching 50 employees is a major milestone for any startup. However, in Spain, it also triggers a set of legal obligations that many founders are unaware of. The most critical of these is the General Disability Law (LGD).

Why 50 Employees?

The LGD stipulates that all companies with 50 or more employees must reserve at least 2% of their workforce for people with a recognized disability. For a company of exactly 50 people, this means at least one individual.

Alternative Measures

We understand that for highly specialized tech startups, finding specific profiles within this quota can sometimes be challenging. The law allows for 'exceptional measures' if you can prove that you've tried to hire but failed, or if you have organizational reasons.

These measures include contracting services with Special Employment Centers (CEE) or making donations to social foundations. Astartes IT helps you navigate this documentation to ensure you stay compliant without slowing down your growth.

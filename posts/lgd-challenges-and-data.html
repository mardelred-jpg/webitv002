Why LGD remains a real challenge for businesses

2025-03-01 4 min read

The legal framework around disability and employment in Spain is mature and extensive. However, the labor reality shows that inclusion is still not fully achieved.

What the data shows

According to the SEPE report with 2023 data, there were 139,321 unemployed people with disabilities registered in Spain. The same report shows that 57.09% of unemployed people with disabilities were in long-term unemployment, which indicates persistent barriers beyond formal legal recognition.

Law and reality do not always match

The law promotes equality, accessibility, action measures and labor inclusion, but the persistence of long-term unemployment shows that compliance cannot be reduced to theory. This gap is one reason why companies should approach LGD as an active process, not a one-time administrative task.

What smart companies do differently

The most effective approach combines legal review, documentation, hiring strategy, accessibility checks and, where appropriate, alternative measures or supported employment. That is usually more efficient than waiting until the company faces an inspection, tender requirement or internal complaint.

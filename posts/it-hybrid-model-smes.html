The hybrid model: the most realistic formula for many SMEs

2026-03-08 3 min read

In practice, many SMEs are not choosing between "all external" or "all internal", but between different combinations of both models. That is why the hybrid approach is establishing itself as a particularly useful solution for companies that want to maintain internal criteria without taking on the full operational load.

How it works

In a hybrid scheme, the company retains direction, priorities and business knowledge, while an external provider covers areas such as helpdesk, cybersecurity, monitoring, cloud or specialised projects. This distribution allows combining control with elasticity and prevents the IT department from becoming a structure too heavy for the actual size of the SME.

From a business perspective, this option usually offers the best balance between governance, cost and responsiveness. It does not require giving up control, but it also does not require internally hiring all the profiles that modern infrastructure demands today.

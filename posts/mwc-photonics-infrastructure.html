Photonics at MWC 2026: the technology that could reduce power consumption and heat in the new AI era

2026-03-07 4 min read

Among the most technical topics at MWC Barcelona 2026, one of the most strategic was photonics applied to networks, servers and data centres. Although it may not have the visual impact of robotics or consumer devices, it could become one of the most important technologies for sustaining the growth of AI.

The reason is simple: the more models, data and processing that are deployed, the greater the energy consumption of the infrastructure. This increase not only means more electricity costs, but also more thermal density and more cooling demands.

IOWN and photonic-electronic convergence

In this context, NTT focused on IOWN and on photonic-electronic convergence as the foundation for a more efficient infrastructure. The idea is to leverage optical technologies to move and process information with fewer losses, less consumption and less heat generation than in traditional architectures dominated by electrical interconnection.

Additionally, the official congress agenda included a session dedicated to the transition "from AI ambition to infrastructure reality", where all-photonics networks were presented as a viable alternative to meet the new digital demands.

Beyond models and software

For a business or technology reader, the conclusion is very powerful: the AI revolution will not be played out solely in models and software, but also in how we connect servers, move data and reduce the energy cost of computing. Photonics appeared in Barcelona as one of the most serious answers to that challenge.

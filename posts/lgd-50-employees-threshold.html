LGD: What changes when your company reaches 50 employees

2025-03-06 4 min read

Reaching 50 employees is a major milestone for any company. In Spain, it also activates a key legal obligation under the General Disability Law: the duty to reserve part of the workforce for people with a recognized disability.

What is the 2% quota?

Spanish law requires public and private companies with 50 or more employees to reserve at least 2% of their workforce for workers with a recognized disability. In practical terms, if your company has 50 employees, you should normally have at least one person within that quota.

Why this matters

The rule is not only about formal compliance. The General Disability Law is built around equality of opportunity, non-discrimination, accessibility and real inclusion in employment. That means the obligation should be understood as part of a broader compliance and inclusion strategy, not just a headcount exercise.

What companies should review

Before crossing the 50-employee threshold, businesses should review their total headcount, hiring processes, internal documentation and recruitment practices. This helps avoid reactive decisions and allows the company to plan either direct hiring or, where legally justified, alternative measures.

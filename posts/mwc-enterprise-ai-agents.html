Enterprise AI at MWC 2026: from conversational assistants to agents that act on processes

2026-03-07 4 min read

One of the most significant changes at MWC Barcelona 2026 was the evolution of enterprise artificial intelligence. It is no longer just about chatbots, automated responses or content generation. The new direction points towards intelligent agents capable of intervening in processes, automating operations and delivering direct business value.

This trend was reflected in proposals oriented towards AI-powered enterprise services, where the goal is no longer just to improve user interaction, but to transform how an organization works internally. NTT, for example, presented solutions based on AI-driven services and its Smart AI Agent concept as a tool for modernizing enterprise operations and workflows.

New evaluation criteria

The importance of this shift is that it changes the evaluation criteria. AI is no longer measured by its ability to "speak well" and is now measured by its ability to produce results: reduce costs, accelerate tasks, improve support, increase efficiency and help in decision-making.

Another important takeaway from the congress is that companies are focusing their efforts on high-value scenarios. This means investments are being directed towards areas where AI can generate clear and measurable impact, such as customer service, maintenance, network operations, data analysis and internal automation.

From experimentation to execution

For businesses, this change is especially relevant because it signals a transition from experimentation to execution. The conversation in Barcelona did not revolve around "trying AI", but around integrating it into the regular functioning of the organization. The conclusion is simple: enterprise AI in 2026 is already thought of as a structural productivity tool.

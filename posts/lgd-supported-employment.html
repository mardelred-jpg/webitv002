Supported employment: the legal path designed to facilitate real inclusion

2025-03-02 3 min read

The legal framework does not only impose obligations. It also creates tools to make hiring more viable, especially for people with greater difficulties of labor insertion.

What is supported employment?

Supported employment is a regulated program designed to help people with disabilities access the ordinary labor market through individualized guidance and on-the-job support. The support is delivered by specialized job coaches and is focused on real integration in ordinary companies, not separate environments.

Why it matters for employers

This model is useful when a company wants to hire but needs structure, accompaniment and a more realistic onboarding process. It is especially relevant for profiles facing greater barriers to labor market access, which the law and policy framework increasingly recognize explicitly.

Inclusion with operational support

For employers, supported employment can reduce uncertainty and improve retention. For compliance, it also aligns with the legal preference for inclusion in the ordinary labor market whenever possible.

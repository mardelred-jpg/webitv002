LGD is not just a quota: it also requires equality, accessibility and non-discrimination

2025-03-04 4 min read

Many companies associate the LGD only with the 2% hiring quota. That is incomplete. The law also protects equality of treatment, accessibility and the effective exercise of rights by people with disabilities.

What does the law actually protect?

The General Disability Law defines disability in relation to the barriers that limit full and effective participation in society. It also includes concepts such as direct discrimination, indirect discrimination, discrimination by association, harassment and positive action measures.

What this means for companies

In practice, compliance goes beyond hiring numbers. It affects recruitment processes, communication, workplace accessibility, digital services, internal policies and how the company responds when a worker needs support or adaptation.

A broader compliance approach

A company may meet the quota and still have weak compliance if barriers remain in access, processes or working conditions. A better approach is to connect legal compliance with operational accessibility and inclusive HR procedures.
